Silencio Por Favor

11 / 02 / 19

Recently, I have come to realise my addiction to my phone - well, not so much an addiction, but I just use it rather a lot. To reduce this, I turned my phone to silent, and oh boy does it feel good. I used to pick up my phone whenever I heard a buzz or a ba-ding. Now, I look at it when I want to. It's only a very small change, but the results have felt rather promising. Other types to reduce screen usage, which I have read, include changing the colours on your phone and limiting the amount that you charge it. I would highly recommend trying out one of the methods, if you are looking to decrease your addiction, too.

Good films are made of these...

18 / 02 / 19

Good films. We each have our own set of favourite films, which we love for different reasons. But, at it's core, I think there is one main keystone thing that determines a good film for the rest: good films make you want to do something. That is, for me, after I watched 'The Social Network' (which I talked about in my very first blog post), I frantically wanted to create the next big social media hit. My brain was wirring with thoughts and ideas that could be the next thing to blow up and become a smash hit. Another example, which was the factor that made me realise all of this, is 'Creed'. 'Creed' is a boxing movie, starring Michael B. Jordan, and if you haven't watched it, I would highly highly recommend you do ASAP. After watching 'Creed', the first thing I did was walk around my house, shadow boxing, whilst thinking about how breathtaking the movie was. It was so good, in fact, that the next day, I watched 'Creed II' - which I would recommend, if you liked the first one. Anyway, the point that I am trying to get at, here, is that if you came away from a movie, wanting to do something, then I would say that movie would be a good movie. Obviously there are many many exceptions and flaws to this rule, but I'd say that it works - at least for the most part.
